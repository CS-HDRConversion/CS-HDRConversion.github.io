Research Area
Cyber Security
Project Description
Many concerns have been voiced about the vulnerability of data-intensive machine learning (ML) algorithms to privacy leakage, due to the attacks revealed in recent years, such as data stealing attacks (i.e., model inversion) and sensitive attributes inference. The auditable and verifiable methods for preserving privacy in ML systems, in this regard, represent an important challenge.
This project aims to develop an auditing framework to conduct a systematic and rigorous assessment of privacy risks of ML, and design and implement innovative machine learning approaches that provide verifiable privacy protection on training data while the performance of training process and the quality of the model can be assured.

Project Overview:

The "Empowering Success" project is dedicated to leveling the educational playing field for socially disadvantaged students through the strategic application of artificial intelligence (AI) and learning analytics. By offering scholarships to eligible students who can enroll this year, we aim to mitigate financial barriers and foster an environment where every student has the opportunity to thrive. This initiative integrates AI-driven tools and learning analytics to tailor the educational experience, enhancing academic outcomes and facilitating the successful completion of studies.

Objectives:

To Develop AI-Driven Learning Solutions: Leverage large language models and intelligent agents to offer personalized tutoring, mentorship, and academic assistance, catering to the unique learning preferences and requirements of socially disadvantaged students.

To Implement Learning Analytics: Employ learning analytics to monitor and evaluate students' progress, engagement, and challenges systematically, enabling effective and timely support interventions.

"Empowering Success" aims to significantly narrow the educational attainment gap for socially disadvantaged students, with expected outcomes including improved retention rates, better academic performance, and the empowerment of students to reach their highest potential. Project Title
Reverse modelling using machine learning
Supervisor
A/Prof Nico Pietroni
Supervisor's profile webpage
https://www.nicopietroni.com
Email
nico.pietroni@uts.edu.au
Scholarship Information
Students will be considered for the UTS competitive scholarship scheme (application deadlines apply).
Project Type
This is a PhD project
Student Residency Requirement
This project is for both Domestic and International students
Research Area
Computer Graphics and AI
Project Description
Modern 3D sensor technologies allow efficient digital reproductions of many real-world objects. However, the produced 3D data is usually organized as a point cloud or a triangle mesh. While this data may accurately represent an object's shape, it is unsuitable for most applications beyond simple viewing. For example, production pipelines like CG animation, games, Computer-Aided-Design or Architecture require polygonal meshes to model the structure and function of the object as well as its surface shape. These compact, highly informative 3D assets are referred to as Base Meshes.

The automatic conversion of a 3D scanned real-world object to a suitable base mesh is a complex and intriguing open problem. Creating a base mesh that effectively captures these semantic properties and is not cluttered with irrelevant features is known as reverse modelling or Re-topology. Such processes convert input 3D data into a more readable, usable, and abundant structure in semantics. Despite significant efforts, state-of-the-art automatic methods still produce results that are considerably different from ones modelled by artists. As a result, base meshes are always manually designed by skilled artists, which is time costly and inefficient.

In this project, we will define a new generation of re-meshing techniques that can interpret and optimize the quality of a tessellation by analyzing its similarity to manually modelled meshes. We will use deep learning techniques in concurrence with differential geometry to learn and replicate the subtle bond between a shape and its ideal tessellation.

Email
dilek.ck@uts.edu.au

Project Type
This can be either a PhD project or a Master by Research Project
Student Residency Requirement
This project is for both Domestic and International students
Research Area
Information Systems
Project Description
Strategic planning in response to disasters distinctly differs from strategic planning in everyday situations. Similarly, the use of IT in normal cases does not necessarily work in emergency environments. However, people are becoming more exposed and vulnerable to social and environmental changes, ranging from natural disasters like earthquakes and floods to uncertainties caused by issues related to climate change. Many debates have been about implementing resilience thinking, allowing communities to prepare for possible stresses and shocks. Although frameworks aim to build inclusive resilience strategies fostering participation and engagement, there is limited resilience-related literature on conceptualizing participation in strategic road mapping.

Many natural disasters take place every year with a great impact on society. This project will focus on finding the opportunities and challenges of using digital technologies in natural disasters from the lens of communities. After overviewing what technologies exist, how they are used, what their use brings in, and what information science could offer to improve challenges, the study aims to match them to particular needs arising at disasters. The final report will contribute to increasing the resilience of communities and getting ready for radical climate change.
